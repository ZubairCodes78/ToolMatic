What is OCR?

OCR stands for Optical Character Recognition. It's a technology that converts different types of documents, such as scanned paper documents, PDF files, or images captured by a digital camera, into editable and searchable data. OCR works by analyzing the patterns of light and dark in an image to identify characters and words.

Modern OCR technology has become incredibly accurate, capable of recognizing text in multiple languages, different fonts, and various layouts. Our tool uses Tesseract.js, one of the most powerful open-source OCR engines, to provide you with accurate text extraction directly in your browser.

How OCR Works

OCR technology works through a multi-step process. First, the image is preprocessed to improve text recognition. This includes adjusting contrast, removing noise, and straightening skewed images. The software then segments the image into lines, words, and individual characters.

Each character is then analyzed and compared against a database of known character patterns. Advanced OCR uses machine learning and neural networks to improve accuracy, especially for handwritten text or unusual fonts. The recognized characters are then reconstructed into words and sentences, producing the final text output.

Our OCR tool performs all of this processing directly in your browser using JavaScript, ensuring your images and extracted text remain private and secure. No data is sent to external servers, making our tool ideal for sensitive documents.
